Why

User-friendly isn’t enough.

If you want your digital assets to be accessible to all, you target the sighted and the blind, the hearing and the deaf, the literate and the illiterate, the speakers of all the world’s 7,000 or so languages, the facile typists and the hunters and peckers, and users with 36-inch monitors and hand-helds.

But how? No single group is a proxy for all hard-to-accommodate folks. True, but geezers (a.k.a. “seniors”) come close, because—statistically, anyway—they tend to have below-average vision, hearing, mobility, response times, fluency in major languages, literacy (especially digital), and social mediacy. Indeed, there’s a common informal usability test: “Could my grandmother use it?”

Moreover, geezers are a growing fraction of the world’s customers. Digitally isolating yourself from them may be hazardous to your mission. They need the usual information, services, and goods, plus geezer-specific ones.

Geezer-friendliness applies to maintenance, too. If your digital content and tools are truly geezer-friendly, geezers can use, update, modify, and manage them, both outside and inside your organization.

As you develop new digital assets or review your existing ones, consider choosing experts who have strength in geezer-friendly design, testing, development, and documentation.

At geezer.pro™, we work to make digital assets truly geezer-friendly. If we do our job, you won’t need us again. When you want something changed, your geezers can do it.
